## Core Framework Terms / 枠組みの中核用語

### 欠損 (kesson)

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: deficit, lack, "something missing"
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Not mere absence. A subjectively experienced gap with creative potential. The character 欠 means "lack" or "chipped," and 損 means "loss" or "damage." Together they suggest something that was expected to be whole but is experienced as broken or incomplete. This experiential quality is the core of Kesson.
- **Framework location / 枠組み内の位置**: Kesson (core definition)

### 保持 (hoji) / 抱持

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: holding, maintaining, 抱持ing
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Active holding-while-evaluating, not passive retention. The English "抱持" carries connotations of deliberate concealment; the Japanese 保持 is more neutral—simply "maintaining in place." The framework uses both terms: the Japanese for the neutral functional description, the English "抱持" (capitalized) as a technical term.
- **Framework location / 枠組み内の位置**: Holding/Containment (core definition), Layer 3

### 場 (ba) — Stage 1 of creation / 創造の第1段階

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: field, place, space
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Nishida Kitarō's 場所 (basho) refers to a non-dualistic ground that is prior to subject-object separation. 場 in this framework inherits that meaning: the undifferentiated potential from which creation emerges. It is not a container (which implies boundaries) but a ground (which implies openness).
- **Related concept / 関連概念**: Nishida's basho (場所), Heidegger's Lichtung

### 波 (nami) — Stage 2 of creation / 創造の第2段階

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: wave, oscillation
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Not random noise but directed movement arising from kesson. The wave is the reverberation of the gap—it has directionality because it originates from a specific absence.
- **Spinor correspondence / スピノル対応**: Pauli matrices, 720° rotation [M]

### 縁 (en) — Stage 3 of creation / 創造の第3段階

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: encounter, connection, fate, karma
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Buddhist concept of interdependent arising (縁起/engi). Not a causal relationship ("A causes B") but a relational one ("A and B arise together in mutual dependence"). In the creative process, this is the moment when an internal oscillation meets something external—not by causation but by resonance.
- **Related concept / 関連概念**: 縁起 (engi/pratītyasamutpāda), Watsuji's 間柄 (aidagara)

### 渦 (uzu) — Stage 4 of creation / 創造の第4段階

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: vortex, whirlpool, spiral
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: A self-organizing structure that emerges from encounter. Not designed or planned—it forms spontaneously when the conditions (縁) are right. Carries connotations of both beauty and danger (the Japanese whirlpools of Naruto Strait / 鳴門の渦潮).
- **Spinor correspondence / スピノル対応**: Particle emergence [M]

### 束 (taba) — Stage 5 of creation / 創造の第5段階

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: bundle, sheaf, fascicle
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Mathematical resonance with fiber bundles (ファイバー束). Conceptually: what was formless (場), then moving (波), then relational (縁), then self-organizing (渦) finally becomes communicable, shareable, crystallized—but never fully separated from its origin in 場.
- **Spinor correspondence / スピノル対応**: Fermi sea, collective structure [M]

## Aesthetic and Philosophical Terms / 美学的・哲学的用語

### 間 (ma)

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: interval, space-between, pause
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Not emptiness but potentiality. The pregnant pause in music, the empty space in a painting, the silence between words. 間 is where meaning lives—not in the elements but in what separates them. Related to the framework's 抱持: the space where kesson is held.

### 花 (hana) — Zeami's aesthetic concept / 世阿弥の美学概念

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: flower (as aesthetic impact)
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Zeami's 花 is not beauty itself but the aesthetic impact that arises from 抱持. 「秘すれば花」(hisureba hana)—"if hidden, it is the flower." What is revealed too easily loses its power. The flower is not in the showing but in the 抱持ing.
- **Framework location / 枠組み内の位置**: Holding/Containment parallel concept (群盲象チェック)

### 意 (i)

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: intention, will, meaning, mind
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: A pre-cognitive layer of consciousness that operates even in sleep and dreams. Not "thinking" (思考) or "reasoning" (推論) but something more fundamental—the basic orientation of consciousness toward the world. The framework's Layer 0-1 operations correspond to 意.

### 違和感 (iwakan)

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: sense of wrongness, discomfort, unease
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: The felt signal that something doesn't fit. Not cognitive analysis ("this is logically wrong") but embodied response ("something feels off"). This is the phenomenological trigger of kesson—the moment before conceptualization, when the body registers a gap that the mind has not yet named.
- **Framework location / 枠組み内の位置**: Layer 0→1 transition, pre-kesson signal

### 覚悟 (kakugo)

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: resolve, preparedness, readiness
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: Not mere determination but a deep acceptance of consequences, including suffering. 覚 (awareness) + 悟 (enlightenment/understanding). To have 覚悟 is to hold the full weight of uncertainty without flinching—a 抱持 condition at L2-L3.
- **Framework location / 枠組み内の位置**: 抱持の成立条件 (抱持 conditions, L2-L3 layer)

## Relationship Terms / 関係性の用語

### 忍ぶ恋 (shinobu koi)

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: hidden love, endured love
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: From Hagakure. Love that is never expressed, never acted upon, yet sustained. This is 抱持 in its most poetic form—holding an emotion without resolving it, and the holding itself transforms the emotion into something that transcends its original form.
- **Framework location / 枠組み内の位置**: Holding/Containment parallel concept (群盲象チェック)

### 未発の中 (mihatsu no chū)

- **Approximate English / 英語近似**: the state before emotions arise
- **What translation misses / 翻訳で失われるもの**: From the Doctrine of the Mean (中庸). The balanced state before emotional responses emerge—not suppression of emotion but the pre-emotional ground. Corresponds to the threshold between Layer 2 (evaluation) and Layer 3 (抱持) — the "gate of 意" (意のゲート).
- **Framework location / 枠組み内の位置**: Layer 2-3 boundary
